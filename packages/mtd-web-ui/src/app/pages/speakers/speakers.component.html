Verna DeMontigny was born in her parents’ home at Fort Ellice Municipality, Manitoba in a small Metis community known as Fouillard’s Corner. Fouillard’s Corner, although it no longer exists, was located 7.5 miles west of Binscarth, Manitoba. Verna’s parents relocated there from Ste Madeleine, MB in 1939 when the PFRA put in a community pasture for cows.

Verna is the daughter of Jean Louis Fleury and Josephine Marie Ledoux. She was born to a large family totaling 11 siblings; 4 sisters, 5 brothers and 2 brothers from a former marriage.

Verna was fortunate to receive a grade 10 education and later in life go back to school and graduate with Grade 12.

Starting early in her teens, Verna worked many different jobs, cutting post, babysitting for a family of 9 children, housekeeping, and gathering and harvesting foods.

Verna married William John DeMontigny in 1968 and together they had 4 boys and 1 daughter.

As they raised their family, Verna relocated from Fort Ellice, MB to Brandon, MB. Here in Brandon, MB she started a small housecleaning business and at the same time worked as a leather seamstress at Fleece Line Ventures.

Verna’s husband passed away in 1986. Verna became involved in the Manitoba Métis Federation and worked there as a Bingo and Canteen worker. She also worked at Southwest Daycare as cook/cleaner and Childcare Worker for many years. After regretfully retiring from Southwest Daycare, she went on to work as a Membership/Harvesting Clerk at Manitoba Métis Federation.

Maintaining her strong Cultural traditions and her ancestral Language of Michif, Verna - after retiring - became a Michif Language Coordinator for the Southwest Region’s Michif Language Program, a Professor at Brandon University and a Michif Language teacher for Brandon School Division. She has worked with various Michif Language Linguists by transcribing Michif Language material and many other ways to preserve her ancestral Language that she loves so much.

Over the last 20 years, Verna has taught her language in many different places, through a variety of organizations that had projects to teach her Michif language. She has over the years attended many Language conferences to revitalize Language .

Verna has sat on many different boards such as the Southwest Local Management Board, Michif Language Advisory Committee, Southwest Daycare, Grand Valley Local and others.

Verna is also an accomplished craftswoman and a successful Storyteller. She loves beading, making Sashes, rugs, preserving berries, food, and sewing. She loves to work with her hands.

She has extensive knowledge of many subsistence activities that we cherish in the Michif culture. She still harvests today and is well known for her homemade canned goods and jams.

As an accomplished teacher, she has preserved many traditions and oral teachings with family and friends that have had the privilege to know her. She is actively involved with the Little Teaching Lodge, Assiniboine Community College and schools in the Brandon School Division giving culture-related presentations. She is also involved in Culture related events throughout the Southwest Region. As time gives her experience, she shares her knowledge with all.

Verna DeMontigny, born 1951, has preserved the knowledge and ancestral Language that was passed on to her from her parents and ancestors. She has experienced many changes, from being raised in a small Métis Community, living in a log cabin, living the traditional way of life, raising her family as a single parent, going back to school after 32 years and working tirelessly to preserve the Oral teachings and Language that was handed down to her.

Verna believes that language and traditional practices are the Cornerstones of The Metis Culture.

She has shown a stunning example of how adaptability, persistence and strong willpower are tools for success.

Sandra Rose Houle was born on November 13, 1959 in Belcourt, ND to Sam Ferris and Ida Swain. She was lovingly raised by her mother and Oscar Houle. Sandra was raised in Belcourt and completed her education there as well, before attending college.

Sandra was united in marriage to Edward Houle on October 9, 1978 in Bottineau, ND.

Together they had three children, Corbet, Eric, and Lane.

Sandra worked as a teacher throughout the years teaching the Michif language to many children and adults in schools, college, and privately throughout the Turtle Mountain area.

She was working with others on a book to teach the language.

Sandra enjoyed listening to “old time” music, beading, sewing, and guitar. She loved time spent with her family members, especially her grandchildren, and time with her friends.

Sandra will be lovingly remembered and greatly missed.

Sandra Rose Houle: November 13, 1959 - July 21, 2020

Albert Parisien Sr. is a highly accomplished and respected figure in the field of heavy equipment operation and instruction. Throughout his career, he has demonstrated exceptional skill and proficiency in the operation and maintenance of a wide range of heavy machinery and has played a critical role in the success of many major projects. As a supervisor and instructor, he has trained and mentored countless individuals, many of whom have gone on to achieve great success in their own careers.

In addition to his professional accomplishments, Albert is also deeply committed to preserving and promoting his cultural heritage as a master Michif speaker for the Turtle Mountain Living Language project. He has dedicated himself to preserving the language and culture of his community and is widely respected and admired for his efforts.

In his personal life, Albert is a devoted husband to Beverly Parisien and a proud father, grandfather and great-grandfather. He is a deeply family-oriented individual and values spending time with his loved ones. He also enjoys playing bingo and is known for his competitive spirit and sharp mind.

Albert lives in Belcourt, ND on the Turtle Mountain Reservation.

Connie Henry is a Michif raised in Boggy Creek, MB, and presently lives nearby in the community of San Clara. Both Connie’s parents spoke Michif as their mother tongue and originally hailed from what is now known as the Turtle Mountain Reservation in North Dakota. Connie is actively working to reclaim more proficiency in her Michif and is involved in language revitalization activities through the Prairies to Woodlands Indigenous Language Revitalization Circle and her own social media efforts. She's a skilled artist, crafting beautiful Metis sashes alongside her main work and a gifted musician.
